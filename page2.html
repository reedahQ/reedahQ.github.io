MY FAVOURITE COMEDIES

10. NANNY MCPHEE: BIG BANG

Nanny McPhee (2005) is a whimsical family film that tells the story of a widowed father, Mr. Brown, struggling to manage his seven unruly children. Enter Nanny McPhee, a mysterious and magical figure with a knack for discipline. As she uses her unconventional methods to teach the children lessons in behavior, her appearance changes with each lesson learned, reflecting their growing respect and obedience. What begins as chaos transforms into a tale of love, family unity, and acceptance. The film is a delightful blend of charm, humor, and heart. Emma Thompson’s portrayal of the no-nonsense, magical nanny strikes a perfect balance between stern and endearing, while the child actors bring a lively energy to the screen. The imaginative use of magic adds a touch of fantasy that feels fresh and enchanting without being overdone. With its touching themes of family and its clever humor, *Nanny McPhee* stands out as a thoroughly enjoyable, family-friendly film that entertains while delivering meaningful lessons.

9. BEAN: THE ULTIMATE DISASTER

*Mr. Bean*, starring Rowan Atkinson, is a delightful comedy series that follows the misadventures of the titular character, a bumbling, childlike man who stumbles through life with a mix of absurdity and charm. Each episode showcases his knack for turning simple tasks, like visiting the park or going to the dentist, into chaotic but hilarious disasters. Atkinson’s brilliant physical comedy and expressive face elevate the show, relying almost entirely on slapstick and visual gags rather than dialogue. As a film critic, I found *Mr. Bean* refreshing in its simplicity—it's a masterclass in physical comedy, offering timeless humor that appeals to all ages. The show’s universal appeal and Atkinson’s impeccable timing make it a joyful watch, filled with laugh-out-loud moments that never grow old.

8. DUMB AND DUMBER

*Dumb and Dumber* (1994) follows two well-meaning but hilariously dim-witted friends, Lloyd Christmas (Jim Carrey) and Harry Dunne (Jeff Daniels), as they embark on a cross-country road trip to return a briefcase full of money to its owner, unaware that it's part of a criminal ransom plot. The film is a relentless parade of absurd situations, misunderstandings, and slapstick humor, with Carrey and Daniels fully embracing their characters' outrageous lack of intelligence. Despite its over-the-top silliness, the film excels in delivering genuine laughs with its ridiculous gags and impeccable comic timing. As a critic, it’s easy to appreciate how *Dumb and Dumber* taps into pure, unfiltered comedy, making it one of the most enduringly funny films of the 90s. It knows exactly what it is: a celebration of the ridiculous, and it does that brilliantly.

7. COMING TO AMERICA

*Coming to America* (1988) follows Prince Akeem of Zamunda, played by Eddie Murphy, as he travels to Queens, New York, in search of true love, hoping to escape an arranged marriage. Disguising himself as an ordinary man, Akeem and his loyal friend Semmi (Arsenio Hall) experience the culture clash between their royal upbringing and the gritty realities of New York City. Akeem eventually falls for Lisa, a smart and independent woman, but must navigate misunderstandings and his secret identity to win her heart. As a film critic, *Coming to America* is a joyous, feel-good comedy that showcases Eddie Murphy’s versatility and comedic brilliance. The film skillfully balances sharp humor with heartwarming moments, and Murphy’s ability to play multiple characters adds an extra layer of entertainment. The satire of wealth and royalty, combined with the charming love story, gives the film a timeless appeal. Its vibrant energy and clever take on cultural differences make it a comedy classic that's still fresh and enjoyable decades later.

6. LETS BE COPS

*Let’s Be Cops* (2014) is a lighthearted buddy comedy about two aimless friends, Ryan (Jake Johnson) and Justin (Damon Wayans Jr.), who stumble into an accidental adventure when they dress up as police officers for a costume party and are mistaken for the real thing. Enjoying the unexpected respect and excitement, they take their new identities too far, getting tangled in a dangerous case involving gangsters and corrupt cops. While the plot is simple and predictable, the film thrives on the charismatic chemistry between Johnson and Wayans, delivering plenty of laughs and absurd situations. It doesn’t take itself too seriously, and that’s its charm—*Let’s Be Cops* offers a fun, escapist ride with just enough wit and heart to keep you entertained throughout.

5. ALI G INDAHOUSE

*Ali G Indahouse* (2002) is a comedy centered around Ali G, a clueless and flamboyant streetwise character, who unexpectedly finds himself in the British Parliament after being manipulated by corrupt politicians as a pawn. Ali, played by Sacha Baron Cohen, navigates political chaos with his outrageous behavior, unaware of being used to bring down the Prime Minister. However, his ridiculous antics inadvertently win over the public and save the day in an absurdly comical fashion. As a film critic, *Ali G Indahouse* is an irreverent and wildly entertaining ride. Sacha Baron Cohen’s fearless commitment to his outlandish character is what makes the film so enjoyable, as it gleefully pokes fun at political absurdities and class stereotypes. While the humor is far from subtle, it’s this boldness that makes the movie work, delivering laugh-out-loud moments with sharp social satire lurking beneath the surface. It may not be for everyone, but for fans of crude humor and satirical jabs, it's a refreshing dose of comedy that doesn’t take itself too seriously.

4. THE THREE STOOGES

The *Three Stooges* (2012) brings the iconic slapstick comedy trio—Larry, Moe, and Curly—back to life in a modern setting. The film follows their wild antics as they try to save their childhood orphanage, stumbling into outrageous situations, from becoming reality TV stars to inadvertently getting mixed up in a murder plot. The Farrelly Brothers’ homage to the original Stooges does justice to their classic physical comedy, blending old-school slapstick with contemporary humor. While the plot is thin, the joy of watching Sean Hayes, Will Sasso, and Chris Diamantopoulos nail the physicality and timing of the original characters makes it an unexpectedly fun and faithful adaptation. It's silly, absurd, and exactly what fans of the original trio would hope for—a loving, laugh-out-loud tribute to a bygone era of comedy.

3. BABYS'S DAY OUT

*Baby's Day Out* (1994) is a lighthearted family comedy about Baby Bink, an infant who gets kidnapped by three bumbling criminals but inadvertently outsmarts them as he embarks on an adventure across the city. As the kidnappers chase him from one dangerous situation to the next, Baby Bink innocently navigates construction sites, a zoo, and traffic, all while the crooks find themselves in increasingly absurd predicaments. The film’s charm lies in its slapstick humor and the innocent, mischievous nature of Baby Bink, while Joe Mantegna and his fellow villains deliver delightfully goofy performances. Though far-fetched, the film is pure fun, with a heartwarming ending that emphasizes family. It’s an enjoyable ride that doesn’t take itself too seriously and provides wholesome entertainment for all ages.

2. HOME ALONE 1

*Home Alone* (1990) follows eight-year-old Kevin McCallister, who is accidentally left behind when his family flies to Paris for Christmas. Initially thrilled to have the house to himself, Kevin’s fun is interrupted when two bumbling burglars, Harry and Marv, attempt to rob his home. Using his quick wit and a series of clever traps, Kevin defends the house, turning the robbers’ attempts into a series of comedic disasters. *Home Alone* is a delightful holiday classic that perfectly balances humor, heart, and slapstick. Macaulay Culkin shines as Kevin, delivering an endearing and mischievous performance that carries the film. The charm lies in its simple, high-concept premise, which director Chris Columbus executes with a great blend of physical comedy and sentimentality. The film’s lighthearted fun, memorable characters, and iconic booby-trap sequences make it a feel-good family movie that remains timeless, delivering laughs and holiday cheer in equal measure.

1. WHITE CHICKS

*White Chicks* is a 2004 comedy that follows FBI agents Kevin and Marcus Copeland, played by Marlon and Shawn Wayans, who go undercover to protect the wealthy heiresses Brittany and Tiffany Wilson after a failed kidnapping attempt. When the Wilson sisters suffer minor injuries, the brothers disguise themselves as the women, leading to a series of hilarious and absurd situations that poke fun at class, race, and identity. This outrageous film is a delightful blend of slapstick humor and social commentary, showcasing the Wayans' comedic talents with a clever premise that keeps audiences engaged. The outrageous makeup and performances are both impressive and funny, highlighting the absurdity of the situations the characters find themselves in. Despite its over-the-top scenarios, *White Chicks* manages to deliver genuine laughs while also offering a satirical take on the superficiality of high society. Its ability to balance humor with social critique makes it a memorable comedy that continues to entertain viewers years after its release.
