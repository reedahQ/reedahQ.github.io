MY FAVOURITE SERIES

10. YOUNG SHELDON

Summary: Young Sheldon is a prequel to The Big Bang Theory, centered around the childhood of Sheldon Cooper, a socially awkward genius growing up in Texas. It explores his family dynamics and his struggles as a gifted child in an ordinary world. Review: Young Sheldon offers a heartwarming and refreshing take on the origins of one of TV’s most beloved characters. I thoroughly enjoyed the blend of humor, family warmth, and emotional depth as we see Sheldon navigate his genius in the relatable, quirky world of his family. The young actor, Iain Armitage, gives a brilliant performance that adds a layer of innocence and charm to the already well-established character.

9. JENIFA

Jenifa's Diary is a Nigerian comedy series that follows the quirky and endearing character of Jenifa, played by Funke Akindele, as she navigates life with her unique blend of innocence, ambition, and outrageous antics. Originally from a small village, Jenifa moves to the bustling city of Lagos with big dreams and a strong, often misguided, determination to succeed. Her misadventures touch on universal themes of love, friendship, and resilience, all while providing a humorous take on Nigerian society and culture. Akindele's performance is exceptional, bringing heart and humor to a character whose linguistic mix-ups and exaggerated expressions make her unforgettable. The show combines comedy with subtle social commentary, addressing real-life issues like self-esteem, peer pressure, and the pursuit of success, wrapped in laugh-out-loud moments and relatable experiences. *Jenifa's Diary* is a delightful, feel-good series that’s both entertaining and impactful, proving that comedy can carry a message without losing its charm. For anyone looking to dive into Nigerian pop culture, this series is a must-watch.

8. SPONGEBOB SQUAREPANTS

Summary: Spongebob Squarepants follows the adventures of the optimistic and energetic Spongebob and his quirky friends in the underwater city of Bikini Bottom.
Review: Spongebob Squarepants is an absolute joy to watch. Its quirky humor, zany characters, and surreal storytelling make it one of the most creative animated series ever. Every episode is a delightful blend of childlike wonder and subtle adult humor, keeping audiences of all ages entertained. Spongebob’s infectious positivity and the show's colorful animation make this a timeless classic that never gets old.

7. LEGACIES

Summary: Legacies is the third series in The Vampire Diaries universe, focusing on Hope Mikaelson, the daughter of Klaus Mikaelson, as she navigates life at the Salvatore School for the Young and Gifted. The show balances high school drama with magical creatures, including witches, werewolves, and vampires. Review: Legacies is a delightful continuation of the supernatural universe, mixing the emotional intensity of its predecessors with a lighter, more adventurous tone. I enjoyed how the show introduced new mythical creatures and expanded the magical lore while still maintaining a strong emotional core through Hope’s character. The youthful energy and diverse cast give the series a fresh yet familiar feeling, perfect for fans of supernatural adventures.

6. THE ORIGINALS

Summary: A spin-off from The Vampire Diaries, The Originals focuses on the Mikaelson family, the original vampires, in the heart of New Orleans. The show explores the family’s complex relationships and their battle for power in a city crawling with witches, vampires, and werewolves. Review: The Originals takes the rich mythology from The Vampire Diaries and intensifies it with more mature themes of power, family loyalty, and betrayal. The dark, gothic setting of New Orleans adds a moody atmosphere to the intense storylines. I found myself invested in the characters' layered and often morally ambiguous arcs, making the series a gripping, emotional rollercoaster. It's a beautifully complex tale about family and survival.

5. SMALLVILLE

Summary: Smallville chronicles the teenage years of Clark Kent before he becomes Superman. The show explores his early struggles with his powers, his relationships with friends and enemies, and the path that eventually leads him to his destiny as Earth's greatest hero. Review: Smallville is a beloved classic that reimagines the Superman mythos with a fresh, youthful lens. Watching Clark Kent’s coming-of-age story unfold over the series was both nostalgic and exhilarating. The show balances superhero action with heartfelt moments of self-discovery, friendship, and family. I especially enjoyed how it built Clark’s relationships with iconic characters like Lex Luthor, making it one of the most enjoyable superhero origin stories on TV.

4. TEEN WOLF

Summary: Teen Wolf follows Scott McCall, a high school student who is bitten by a werewolf and must balance his new supernatural abilities with the pressures of teenage life, friendships, and romance, all while defending his town from dark forces. Review: Teen Wolf is a perfect blend of supernatural action and teenage drama that delivers both thrills and heart. The show excels in character development, with Scott’s journey from an ordinary teenager to a powerful leader being particularly compelling. The fast-paced action sequences, combined with the emotional weight of friendships and relationships, kept me invested from start to finish. It’s an exciting, emotional ride that masterfully blends the supernatural with the everyday struggles of adolescence.

3. PEAKY BLINDERS

Summary: Set in post-World War I Birmingham, Peaky Blinders follows the rise of the Shelby crime family led by the ambitious Tommy Shelby. The series is a gritty look at power, crime, and family, with sharp writing and unforgettable characters. Review: Peaky Blinders is a stylish and brutal portrayal of ambition, family loyalty, and survival in the harsh world of organized crime. Cillian Murphy delivers a powerhouse performance as Tommy Shelby, leading a show full of tension, intrigue, and breathtaking cinematography. The show’s period setting is impeccably detailed, and the slow-burn storylines kept me on the edge of my seat throughout. It’s a masterpiece of modern television.

2. LUCIFER

Summary: Lucifer follows the Devil himself, Lucifer Morningstar, as he abandons Hell and starts a new life in Los Angeles, running a nightclub and helping the LAPD solve crimes. Over time, he grapples with his own identity, redemption, and complex relationships with both humans and celestial beings. Review: Lucifer is a clever, witty, and often surprisingly heartfelt show that offers a fun twist on the classic crime procedural genre. Tom Ellis is perfectly cast as the charming and conflicted Lucifer, and the blend of humor, crime-solving, and existential questions about good and evil keeps the show fresh and engaging. It’s a devilishly fun series with moments of deep introspection and emotional payoff.

1. VAMPIRE DIARIES

Summary: The Vampire Diaries follows the story of Elena Gilbert, who finds herself torn between two vampire brothers, Stefan and Damon Salvatore, in the mysterious town of Mystic Falls. As the series progresses, we see an intense mixture of love, danger, and supernatural intrigue. Review: With its intoxicating blend of romance, danger, and supernatural lore, The Vampire Diaries is a thrilling ride from start to finish. I loved how it seamlessly combines dark themes with light-hearted moments, while the chemistry between the cast elevates the show into a must-watch. The world-building and mythology are top-notch, keeping you hooked as the characters evolve, often blurring the lines between hero and villain.
